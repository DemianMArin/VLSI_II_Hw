  When adding the output capacitances. The rising/falling times for Static and Domino logic got affected. This meant that in order to compensate a ratio of 4:4 and 4:2 for Static and Domino respectively. \\

  This helped to reduce propagation delay in both High-to-Low and Low-to-High for static and domino. But this also affected the energy consumed. From Tables \ref{tab:p1_metricsTable} and \ref{tab:p1_energyTable} and from the simulations in Figures \ref{fig:p1_staticCadence}, 
  \ref{fig:p1_dynamicCadence}, \ref{fig:p1_dominoCadence} it can clearly be seen that Dynamic Logic dominates in all metrics. It has the lowest average propagation delay (as well as in the individual $t_{HL}$ and $t_{LH}$). The next best type of logic is

  The integral for the calculating the Switching Energy was done from 10ps to 110ps given that the clk period was of 100ps with a delay of 10ps at the input.

  \subsection*{v)}
  Lower Vdd and higher temperature causes the transistors to switch slower. Lower Vdd decreases $V_{OV}$ overdrive voltage, decreasing $I_{D}$, slowing down charging and discharging. Something similar happens with higher temperature, which reduces carrier
  mobility, reducing also charging and discharging. So if the transistors switch slower, hold time and setup time increase. Meaning the transistors need more time to charge but there is also a wider window in which D can change before the clk. \\
  The opposite happens with Increase of Vdd and lower temperatures. Setup and hold time reduce.
  
  An example can be seen in Figure \ref{fig:p2_less_vdd} where the setup time increased to $52ps$ and hold time to $5ps$.
